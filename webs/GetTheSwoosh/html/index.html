ABOUT US

Get The Swoosh is an online marketplace and sneakers reseller, primarily of swoosh sneakers. The Detroit-based company was founded by Dan Gilbert, Josh Luber, Greg Schwartz, and Chris Kaufman during 2015–2016. Get The Swoosh has more than 800 employees in Downtown Detroit. Get The Swoosh currently has international offices in London, UK, in Eindhoven, the Netherlands, and has authentication facilities in Detroit's Corktown neighborhood, Moonachie, NJ, and Tempe, AZ. Scott Cutler and Schwartz serve as chief executive officer and chief operating officer, respectively, and Deena Bahri became the company's first chief marketing officer in 2019

Sneakers of the week

Nike Air Max 90 Paris

The Nike Air Max 90 “Paris” is a special iteration that is a nod to Parisian bakers for their artistry and work ethic. Taking cues from the traditional chef’s uniform, the runner is treated in a clean white finish. On the tongue and heel, modified Nike Air branding incorporates the French flag and an embroidered illustration of wheat. The insole includes gluten-infused drawings and a graphic mimicking traditional bakery packaging, complete with a list of ingredient, while Sport blue embroidery on the inside of the tongue rounds out the standout theme.

Go.
Travis Scott x Air Jordan 1 Low Dark Mocha

There is still no release date, but official images of the Travis Scott x Air Jordan 1 Low Dark Mocha have finally been unveiled. Does that mean that a drop is right around the corner? Stay woke! Designed as a low-top rendition of the aforementioned AJ 1 High, it sports an inverted color scheme of Black, Dark Mocha, University Red, and Sail instead. Black underlays with dark brown nubuck overlays comprise the upper, while the model is topped off with enlarged reversed white Swooshes on the outer lateral sides, followed by red accents throughout via Cactus Jack branding and the wings logo on the heel tabs.

Go.
Nike Kobe 5 Protro Big Stage

The Nike Kobe 5 Protro “Big Stage” takes elements from both the original Home and Away pairs that Kobe Bryant wore in the 2010 NBA Finals – “Big Stage” and “Parade” PE. Defined by a split motif design, the lateral and medial sides are essentially different from each other. The “Big Stage Home” motif covers the medial side, while the laser etching design from the “Parade” pair is noted on the lateral. Finally, gold accents and branding throughout round out the style.

Go.
Air Jordan 3 Retro OG Black Cement 2018

This Air Jordan 3 features a Black tumbled leather dominating the upper with additional black accents on the laces and midsole. Cement Grey can then be seen on the tongue, inner liner, ankle, and outsole. Finishing details include Fire Red on the inner tongue and the Jumpman on the tongue, the signature black and grey elephant print on the toe and heel, Nike branding on the insoles and outsoles, and the beloved Nike Air branding on the heel in white.

Go.
Nike Dunk Low Plum 2020

Having originally released in 2001, the Nike Dunk Low Plum will be returning for the first time ever 19 years later. This classic colorway of the lowtop Nike Dunk comes constructed out of a full suede upper in light purple with dark purple detailing on the overlays. For further contrast we see dark red on the Swooshes, laces, heel tab, the Nike branding on the tongue, and rubber outsole

Air Jordan 6 Alternate

This latest variation of the Air Jordan 6 is an extension of Jordan Brand‘s “Alternate” collection. Somewhat of a hybrid maroon variation as well, the sneaker pays homage to the Chicago Bulls’ color scheme with white, pure platinum and gym red accents covering its entire upper. This iteration is also the pair that MJ would’ve donned during the 1991 Playoffs, as the year ‘1991’ is evidenced on the tongues. Finally, translucent outsoles finish off the design
